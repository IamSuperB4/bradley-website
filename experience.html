Experience

The majority of my current professional developer experience come from the Kenworth Trucking Company, a subsidiary of PACCAR. I built them a website to track diagnostic information on trucks being built in their plants, and a custom project tracking tool for the advanced manufacturing engineering division.

Kenworth Trucking Co. – Software Developer – Chassis Chaser - Kirkland, WA - August 2019 - December 2022

During my time at the University of Washington, I worked at Kenworth as a contracted software developer. Being a contractor allowed flexibility in my work hours during school. I worked part-time during school, and full-time during breaks.

I was tasked to build a website, called Chassis Chaser, that helped Paccar’s plants around the world have up-to-date diagnostic information about all the trucks on the factory line. I was able to provide a wealth of information to diagnosticians, managers, engineers, and data analysts. Some of the key information I tracked was the VIN, electronic control units (ECUs), their software parts and software version, programming tests completed at stations on the line, and the current location of each chassis. Across all 7 plants there are a few hundred trucks built per day. Thanks to Chassis Chaser, diagnosticians went from searching through a bunch of XML log files manually to just searching for a specific chassis on the website to find the information they needed. A 15–60-minute process turned into a 10 second process, creating immense time savings for people in the plants.

Chassis Chaser has 4 key parts: the Angular frontend, a .NET Core API, a SQL database, and a .NET Core service that runs on a server collecting real-time chassis data. All of this was designed and built from scratch. I also have to thank my mentors from Paccar’s IT department for helping me throughout the process: Matt and Isaac.

The first part of the process was to collect requirements for exactly what data and features the stakeholders wanted. I had meetings with key diagnosticians, testers, managers, and engineers who would use Chassis Chaser on a daily basis. Everyone wanted a variety of features, some feasible and some not, some critical and some miscellaneous. I continued these meetings until we had a list of requirements with priorities and time estimates.
